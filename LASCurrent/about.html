Club ByLaws

I. OFFICIAL NAME

The official name of this organization shall be the Marist College Literary Arts Society.

II. PURPOSE

To promote an environment conducive to literary appreciativeness and support the progression of original literary work through weekly meetings.

To ensure the presence of the club to the surrounding community by volunteering, creating, or supporting programs helpful to the present community.

To give the club members a creative space where they can share and listen to each other's work through bi-monthly workshops.

To publish a literary magazine every semester named The Mosaic.

To provide Marist College with the means to share inter-disciplinary academic work. The monthly publication of the FoxForum aims to allow the students to dispense their ideas beyond the classroom setting, and to learn from the research of their peers.

III. MEMBERSHIP

Qualification of Membership

The club has open membership to interested individuals.

Financial Obligations of Members

No dues are required of club members. Financial needs are met through allocated funds, donations, and club created fundraisers.

Hazing Policy

No form of hazing whatsoever is tolerated by the club. Hazing, as defined by the Marist College Division of Judicial Affairs, is "Any action, which endangers the mental, emotional, or physical health or safety of a student, or which destroys or removes public or private property, for the purpose of initiation, admission into, affiliation with, or as a condition for continued membership in an organization or team whose members are, or include, students at Marist." Failure to recognize this policy will result in disciplinary action.

Provisions for Resignations

If an officer resigns from his/her post before election for the following year is held, the E-board with the leadership of its President and Advisor, must appoint a temporary officer who is within the E-board or dissolve responsibilities accordingly. The temporary officer, if appointed, will reside until an emergency election takes place.

**Please note that emergency elections are conducted only if the E-board balance of responsibilities is severely affected.

Those officials appointed during emergency elections must re-run once standard elections for the following year are held.

IV. OFFICERS

The club has seven different positions: President, Vice President, Mosaic Editor-in-Chief (s), Treasurer, Webmaster/Head of Promotions, Secretary, and FoxForum Editor-in-Chief (s).

Duties

President : This officer chairs E-board meetings and is primarily responsible for attending all SGA meetings. The President must also be present at most if not all general meetings held for the club and coordinate all activities. The President must be informed on all aspects of the club and facilitate a healthy collaboration amongst E-board members and interested outside groups. This officer must draft a club calendar, club budget for their term, and facilitate any revisions of the club BY-LAWS if necessary.

Vice President : This officer should be in attendance at any event that the President will not be able to make. The Vice-President shall also help run and organize major club events. This officer will be in charge of organizing the club's community service activities and of ensuring that the Literary Arts Society plays a role in the community at least once a semester. The Vice President will also be in charge of overseeing the FoxForum prior to its publication.

Mosaic Editor-in Chief (s): Because of the extensive nature of this position, it can, and when possible should be, shared between two people. The officers are responsible for the organization of the club's literary magazine and its artistic development. The Editors are to assure that no material printed in the magazine is offensive to persons based on color, religion or sexual preference. These officers must report progress during E-board meetings and monitor budget allowances for the magazine's publishing. The Editors have creative control but they must still report to President before sending each issue out for publication.

FoxForum Editor-in Chief(s) : This position, too, can be shared between two people. The editors will be responsible for gathering academic papers from a wide range of students to include in each issue of the FoxFurum. They will also have to assemble said papers into a readable document and proofread for grammatical errors. Although they will have a fair amount of freedom in terms of what should go into each issue, they are obliged to report to the Vice President before publication.

Treasurer: This officer is responsible for all dealings with the club's budget. They must inform E-board members of the status of the budget, hand in check requests, purchase orders, and Sodexho catering forms. The Treasurer is responsible for calculating monetary needs in reference to event needs. This officer must also keep a steady record of funding usages.

Webmaster/ Head of Promotions: This officer is responsible for the upkeep of club's website. The officer will help organize or document any events that will enhance the website, i.e. E-board member pictures, collection of calendar of events for updates, etc. This officer is also responsible for the club's promotion team. He or she will be in charge of creating and of distributing flyers/posters/etc. to advertise the club's events.

Secretary: This officer is responsible for sending general messages for all club members via e-mail. He or she reserves rooms for all events and meetings and keeps a record of all meetings as to update advisors or any officer or member who is absent. This person keeps club organized and in contact with one another. The secretary will be in charge of keeping a thorough record of the club's attendance and of organizing the club's priority points at the end of each semester.

Election Procedures

Standard elections are held in the Spring semester each academic year. If a particular position is not elected then it is either appointed or responsibilities dissolved amongst remaining members until deemed inefficient.

All members present at an election will have an equal secret vote. If a tie occurs, the President has the deciding vote.

V. MEETINGS

The club will hold general meetings every other week where attendance is documented and counted towards priority points. Alternate weeks, there will be workshop meetings where attendance is not mandatory. E-board meetings will be held once every two weeks, or as deemed necessary.

VI. COMMITTEES

Presently to encourage overall club participation there are four re-occurring committees. Those are: Promotions, Mosaic, Fox Forum, Set-up/Clean-up.

Promotions

This committee's goal is to post announcements through College Activities about club events and increase the participation of students both within and outside the club. In order to disseminate information about the club's event they might utilize the radio, newspaper, or any other applicable form of promotion in accordance to school guidelines. The Webmaster/Head of Promotions will be in charge of overseeing said committee and making sure it is functional.

Mosaic

This committee helps in all necessary aspects of the magazine and works with the Editor-in-Chief. This includes production, distribution, and editing and soliciting submissions.

FoxForum

This committee will be in charge of gathering information and collecting quirky details to add to the last page of the FoxForum publication. This page is comprised of facts/interviews/tips and other miscellaneous information that is reflective of overall student population of Marist College.

Set-up/Clean-up and Decorations

This committee is in charge of room set-up and food pick-up and drop off. The committee will help with any decorations for major events.

Any other committees may be created with the consensus of the E-board and/or general club members.

VII. AMENDMENTS TO BY-LAWS

These By-Laws may be updated with changing needs of the club. The President will conduct a meeting where E-Board and Advisor are present to discuss any changes. Ultimately the E-Board as a whole will decide upon the fate of the updates and amendments.

VIII. POLICIES AND ACTIONS

The club MUST organize at least one community service event that is mandatory for all club members.

The club MUST also inform general members of priority point system in relation to the club.

The club MUST produce a literary magazine for outside club members.

The club MUST provide an environment that is supportive of difference and communication.

The club and any individual associated with the said club shall abide and conform to all Federal Laws, New York State Laws, all rules and regulations of Marist College, and all directives of the Student Government Association. Any violation of the aforementioned criteria will result in the disciplinary action taken of the club's charter

IX. Priority Points

To earn 1 point: Members must attend minimum of 50% of club/organization meetings for the semester and 50% of club/organization activities.

To earn 2 points: Members must attend minimum of 50% of club/organization meetings for the semester and 75% of club/organization activities PLUS the community service event.

To earn 3 points (officers only): Officers must attend 50% of club/organization meetings for the semester and 100% of club activities (which includes community service event).

If officers do not complete all of the requirements to earn 3 points, they will be eligible to receive up to 2 points based on the criteria outlined above.

The Humarists Bylaws
I. ADDING NEW MEMBERS
Inclusion into the group is decided upon by a unanimous vote among active members after a two-part tryout process.
1.1 Prospective members will be judged on their ability to both perform the art of Improv and to grasp new concepts relating to Improv.
1.2 Day 1 of tryouts is open to all willing participants regardless of proficiency.
1.3 Day 2 of tryouts (call-backs) are only open to those whom have received confirmation from the directors. The decision to include a prospective member for the second tryout is based off of an informal council of current members directly following Day 1.
1.4 Upon inclusion into the group new members must also tryout the next semester for final review by senior group members. Unless extreme circumstances present themselves this is the only time a member can be expelled from the group. Reasons for expulsion can include poor attendance, poor performance, or a general lack of effort.
II. MEMBERSHIP EXPECTATIONS
Members are expected to attend all practices, shows, and group activities unless in event of extenuating circumstances.
2.1 Members are expected to contribute a minimum of one written piece (video or stage sketch) for each Big Show.
2.2 Members are expected to be present and willing participants at workshops prior to tryouts.
2.3 Members are expected to dedicate the required amount of hours necessary to complete video projects prior to the Big Show. In addition if a member is not a direct participant in the video they are expected to offer technical support to the video director.
III. EXECUTIVE POWER
Executive powers are held by the Director(s). All issues that arise within the group are to be decided upon by the Director, but only upon informal consultation from all active group members.
3.1 Directors are chosen by the preceding Director based on responsibility and merit.
3.2 The Director (or a delegate) is responsible for securing locations for practice and shows.
3.3 The Director is responsible for defining the artistic direction of the group.
3.4 The Director may be impeached in the event of extreme circumstances by a unanimous vote among active members.
IV. PRIORITY POINTS
Priority Points are awarded to all members who actively attend practice and shows.
V. HAZING
All existing Humarists traditions are intended to be in good humor and are harmless trust building exercises. If a member has a problem with an existing tradition they do not have to participate in the group, and can leave the group at any time, for these exercises are parts of Humarist tradition.
VI. FACULTY ADVISOR
The Director (or a delegate) is responsible for maintaining an open channel of communication between the group and the selected Faculty Advisor.
6.1 Faculty Advisors can make suggestions to the Director about possible shows or group direction, but ultimate power of decision rests with the Director(s).
